A Study on Typography

Role: Visual Designer
Software Used: Illustrator, InDesign

The Book

This project uses 24 different fonts (12 serif and 12 sans serif) and explores how letters' shapes interact with each other. The goal of this project is to look closely at various fonts and how text as a whole changes based on its size and leading.

Starting Out

The first part of the project focused solely on the form of the fonts. We were to look at the ends of two characters in a font and how they could interact with each other to make a single icon or logo.

Click on the images to expand.

The Book

The second part of this project looked at the optimum leading (line spacing) and sizes for each font. We used InDesign for this section to quickly edit the lines and guides.

Taking Photos

The book's visuals had to have a cohesive theme, and I happened to be a laser cutting monitor at the time. I experimented with various letters made from laser cut materials. I tried cutting letters and placing them next to associated objects I found around the makerspace (S for sawdust, W for wires). Then cut out letters to cover the lenses for custom bokeh. Finally, refined the bokeh idea and took pictures of the string lights at various angles.

Putting It Together

The final step was to put it together into a hundred page guide and have it printed into a book.
