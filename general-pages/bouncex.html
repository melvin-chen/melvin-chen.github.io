Roles: Frontend Developer, QA, Writer, Designer
Software Used: BounceX, VS Code, Google Suite

Overview

I had a lot of roles at BounceX, but my official job was a client engineer in the visual experience team. I would ensure that the experiences built by the designers had the expected functionality and design. The job would entail incorporating the advertisements and promotions in a way that they look as native as possible.

Everyday Clients

In my everyday work, I would work on experiences that would appear on various clients sites. Those clients included (but weren't limited to):

Nike, Uniqlo, Kate Spade, Coach, Meredith, Megabus, and many, many more.

Writing

In my second quarter at BounceX, I mentioned to my supervisor that I wanted to work on my writing skills. While there was plenty of technical writing that could be done, I wanted to do more narrative or personal writing. She suggested that I start writing a blog, or if I wanted to, to write for the BounceX blog. A few emails later, I got my blog published on the BounceX blog!

Design

For one of my Northeastern objectives at this co-ops, I wanted to work in or with the design team to get some exposure to design at BounceX. For one of my company developmental goals (a goal that furthers the company as a whole), I built a Chrome extension that copy and pasted commonly used code snippets and extended it to the design team.
